Tulips

Tulips are iconic spring-blooming flowers that come in a rainbow of vibrant colors. They are a symbol of love and elegance, perfect for gardens or bouquets.

Plant Description

Tulips are bulbous perennials known for their stunning, cup-shaped flowers. They bloom in the spring, creating a magnificent display in gardens and landscapes.

Common Varieties

  • Darwin Hybrid Tulips
  • Triumph Tulips
  • Parrot Tulips
  • Double Late Tulips

Plant Care Tips

  • Light: Requires full to partial sunlight for optimal growth and blooming.
  • Watering: Water thoroughly after planting and keep the soil slightly moist during active growth.
  • Soil: Prefers well-draining, fertile soil with a neutral to slightly acidic pH.
  • Temperature: Plant bulbs in the fall for spring blooms; they require a period of cold dormancy.
  • Fertilizer: Use a bulb-specific fertilizer during planting and early spring.

Fun Facts

Tulips were once more valuable than gold during the "Tulip Mania" of the 17th century in the Netherlands. They remain one of the most beloved flowers worldwide.
