Fritillaria

Fritillaria is a genus of flowering plants in the lily family, known for its striking bell-shaped flowers.

Plant Description

The Fritillaria plant is known for its unique, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, yellow, and red. This plant is often used in garden designs for its ornamental beauty and elegance.

Common Names

  • Fritillary
  • Crown Imperial

Plant Care Tips

  • Light: Full sun to partial shade. The more sun, the better the blooms!
  • Watering: Keep the soil moderately moist but not soggy. It needs well-draining soil.
  • Soil: Well-draining, loamy soil. Fritillaria plants thrive in fertile, rich soil.
  • Temperature: Prefers cooler temperatures (60°F to 70°F), and should be protected from extreme heat.

Fun Facts

Fritillaria flowers have a distinctive, nodding shape and are a favorite of many gardeners for their beauty and versatility in the landscape. These plants are also deer-resistant, making them ideal for gardens in areas with a high deer population.
