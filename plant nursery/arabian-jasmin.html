Arabian Jasmine

Arabian Jasmine is a highly fragrant, tropical plant that produces stunning, star-shaped white flowers. Known for its alluring scent, it is often used in perfumes and teas.

Plant Description

Arabian Jasmine (Jasminum sambac) is an evergreen shrub or vine native to Southeast Asia. It is renowned for its glossy green leaves and intensely fragrant blooms that appear throughout the year in warm climates.

Common Uses

  • Perfume production
  • Flavoring for teas
  • Ornamental purposes in gardens

Plant Care Tips

  • Light: Thrives in bright, indirect sunlight but can tolerate some direct light.
  • Watering: Water regularly, keeping the soil moist but not soggy. Reduce watering in winter.
  • Soil: Prefers well-draining, slightly acidic to neutral soil.

Fun Facts

Arabian Jasmine is the national flower of the Philippines and Indonesia. Its flowers are often used in religious ceremonies and to make fragrant garlands.
