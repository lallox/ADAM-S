Cactus

Cacti are hardy plants that have adapted to thrive in arid environments, making them an excellent choice for low-maintenance gardening.

Plant Description

Cacti are succulents that store water in their thick, fleshy stems. They are well-suited for hot, dry climates and are known for their unique shapes and occasional beautiful blooms.

Common Varieties

  • Golden Barrel Cactus (Echinocactus grusonii)
  • Saguaro Cactus (Carnegiea gigantea)
  • Prickly Pear Cactus (Opuntia)
  • Christmas Cactus (Schlumbergera)

Plant Care Tips

  • Light: Requires plenty of bright, direct sunlight. Place near a sunny window or outdoors in a sunny spot.
  • Watering: Water sparingly. Allow the soil to dry completely between waterings to avoid overwatering.
  • Soil: Use a well-draining cactus mix or add sand/perlite to regular potting soil.
  • Temperature: Thrives in warm temperatures above 50°F (10°C). Protect from frost.
  • Fertilizer: Feed with a cactus-specific fertilizer during the growing season (spring and summer).

Fun Facts

Cacti are native to the Americas, with some species living for over 100 years. Their spines are modified leaves that help reduce water loss and protect them from predators.
