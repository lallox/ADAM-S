Hydrangea

Hydrangeas are enchanting flowering plants known for their lush blooms that can change color based on soil pH. These versatile plants symbolize gratitude and abundance.

Plant Description

Hydrangeas are deciduous shrubs that produce large, ball-shaped clusters of flowers. The color of the blooms can range from pink to blue, depending on the acidity of the soil.

Common Varieties

  • Bigleaf Hydrangea (Hydrangea macrophylla)
  • PeeGee Hydrangea (Hydrangea paniculata)
  • Oakleaf Hydrangea (Hydrangea quercifolia)
  • Climbing Hydrangea (Hydrangea anomala)

Plant Care Tips

  • Light: Thrives in partial to full sunlight, with some shade during the hottest part of the day.
  • Watering: Keep the soil consistently moist but well-drained. Water deeply during dry spells.
  • Soil: Prefers rich, well-draining soil. The pH of the soil affects bloom color (acidic for blue, alkaline for pink).
  • Pruning: Prune after flowering to maintain shape and encourage healthy growth.
  • Fertilizer: Use a balanced fertilizer in spring and early summer.

Fun Facts

Hydrangeas can "remember" the acidity of their soil and adapt their bloom color accordingly. They are often used in landscaping for their dramatic and eye-catching flowers.
