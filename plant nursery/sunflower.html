Sunflower (Helianthus annuus)

Sunflowers are one of the most iconic and cheerful flowers, known for their bright yellow petals and large, sunny faces. They are easy to grow and bring vibrancy to any garden.

Plant Description

Sunflowers (Helianthus annuus) are large, annual plants with a bright yellow bloom that follows the movement of the sun, hence their name. These cheerful flowers are native to North America and are a symbol of positivity.

Common Names

  • Sunflower
  • Helianthus

Plant Care Tips

  • Light: Sunflowers thrive in full sun. Ensure they receive at least 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight per day.
  • Watering: Water regularly, but be careful not to overwater. Allow the soil to dry slightly between waterings.
  • Soil: Prefers well-draining, slightly acidic to neutral soil.
  • Temperature: Sunflowers prefer warm temperatures and should be planted after the last frost when the weather is consistently warm.

Fun Facts

Sunflowers not only brighten up gardens, but they are also known to have a unique ability to remove toxins from the soil, a process known as phytoremediation.
