Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis miller)

Aloe Vera is a succulent plant known for its healing properties, especially its gel, which is commonly used for soothing burns, cuts, and promoting skin health.

Plant Description

Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis miller) is a popular medicinal plant that stores water in its fleshy leaves. The gel inside these leaves is commonly used for skin care, wound healing, and digestive health.

Common Names

  • Aloe Vera
  • Burn Plant
  • First Aid Plant

Plant Care Tips

  • Light: Aloe Vera thrives in bright, indirect sunlight. It can tolerate some direct sunlight but should be protected from harsh midday sun.
  • Watering: Water the plant deeply but infrequently. Let the soil dry completely between waterings to avoid root rot.
  • Soil: Prefers well-draining, sandy or loamy soil.
  • Temperature: Aloe Vera grows best in warm climates and should be protected from frost.

Fun Facts

Aloe Vera gel is not only great for treating sunburns but is also used in the cosmetic and health industries due to its moisturizing and healing properties.
