Lilies

Lilies (Lilium) are beautiful and fragrant flowering plants, cherished for their elegance and symbolic meaning.

Plant Description

Lilies are perennial plants known for their tall stems, lance-shaped leaves, and trumpet-shaped flowers. They come in various colors, including white, pink, yellow, orange, and red, making them a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Common Names

  • True Lily
  • Asiatic Lily
  • Oriental Lily
  • Easter Lily

Plant Care Tips

  • Light: Prefers full sun to partial shade, with at least 6 hours of sunlight daily.
  • Soil: Thrives in well-drained, slightly acidic soil enriched with organic matter.
  • Watering: Keep the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged. Water deeply once or twice a week.
  • Fertilizer: Apply a balanced fertilizer every 4-6 weeks during the growing season.
  • Winter Care: Cover the bulbs with mulch in colder climates to protect them from freezing temperatures.
Fun Facts

In Greek mythology, lilies are believed to have originated from the milk of Hera, the queen of the gods. The white lily is a symbol of purity and innocence, often associated with the Virgin Mary in Christian traditions.
